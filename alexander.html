Alexander the Great

Early Life

Alexander the Great was born in 356 BC in Pella, the ancient capital of Macedonia. He was the son of King Philip II and Queen Olympias.

Conquests

Alexander is known for his unprecedented military campaigns, which led to the creation of one of the largest empires in history, stretching from Greece to Egypt and into northwest India.

Legacy

Alexander's legacy includes the spread of Greek culture and influence throughout the known world, a period known as the Hellenistic Era. He is often regarded as one of history's greatest military minds.
